Where to Go in the UK

Classy towns, old cities, castles, and a few phwoar-worthy vistas. This isn't everything, but if you can only pick a few different places to stay, here's how I'd cluster it from North to South to get the most out of the trip.

My suggested route

Stay in 5 different places and hit the best of the UK. There are some honourable mentions at the bottom too.

Region III

Cotswolds

Villages, towns, and palaces, oh my!

Cotswolds

The flagship stop (not biased)

Architecture runs from 70AD to the regency period. Roman roads. A few of the oldest pubs in the world. Spa towns and cities. All easily reachable by car.

Cotswolds

Blenheim Palace

Just crazy, honestly. Absolutely vast and the inside is like Versailles.

Honourable mentions

Very good, just not in the main route

These are the places that missed the core itinerary because they're miles out the way rather than because they're bad.

Honourable mention

Lake District

Stunning and worth seeing - just a bit more hike-y. Plus, once you've done 1 walk you've kind of got the gist of it.

Honourable mention

Snowdonia

Very north-of-the-wall, but again more of a hiking spot and not really within reach of much else.

Honourable mention

Portmeirion

Definitely the prettiest town in Wales. It's like a film set, but well out the way with nothing else around.

Honourable mention

New Forest

Wild ponies, heathland, villages, and lots of walking, but again there's nothing else in the vicinity.
